Key DDoS Incident Response Steps

1. Preparation

Establish contacts, define procedures, and gather tools to save time during an attack.

2. Analysis

Detect the incident, determine its scope, and involve the appropriate parties.

3. Mitigation

Mitigate the attack’s effects on the targeted environment.

4. Wrap-Up

Document details, discuss lessons learned, and adjust plans and defenses.

General Considerations

  • DDoS attacks often flood the network with unwanted traffic or overwhelm specific system resources.
  • Defending effectively usually requires specialized equipment or ISP assistance.
  • Limit the incident response team size to avoid chaos.
  • Attacks can last days—plan for team fatigue and shifts.
  • Understand your equipment’s real DDoS mitigation capabilities.

Prepare for a Future Incident

  • Contact your ISP about available DDoS mitigation options and activation processes.
  • Create a whitelist of critical IPs/protocols to prioritize during an attack.
  • Lower DNS TTLs on key systems for faster failover.
  • Establish contacts: ISP, law enforcement, internal teams.
  • Document infrastructure, topology, and asset inventory.
  • Assess business impact of potential DDoS scenarios.
  • Consider dedicated DDoS mitigation services if risk is high.
  • Harden network, OS, and application configurations.
  • Baseline normal performance for faster attack detection.
Next: Analysis Phase

Analyze the Attack

  • Map the logical flow and identify affected components.
  • Review logs and loads on servers, routers, firewalls, and applications.
  • Identify distinguishing characteristics of malicious traffic.
  • Use network analyzers (tcpdump, NetFlow, etc.).
  • Coordinate with ISP and internal teams for visibility and assistance.
  • Check for extortion demands preceding the attack.
  • Create NIDS signatures if possible.
  • Notify executives/legal; consider law enforcement involvement.
Next: Mitigation

Mitigate the Attack’s Effects

  • Block/throttle traffic as upstream as possible.
  • Tune TCP/IP settings and terminate unwanted connections.
  • Fail over to alternate sites/networks via DNS.
  • Blackhole traffic to affected IPs.
  • Temporarily disable bottleneck features.
  • Add capacity (servers/bandwidth) if feasible.
  • Route through scrubbing services.
  • Make one defensive change at a time.
  • Implement egress filtering to reduce outbound amplification.
Next: Wrap-Up

Wrap-Up the Incident and Adjust

  • Review what pre-incident preparation would have helped.
  • Update assumptions and plans based on lessons learned.
  • Evaluate response process and communications.
  • Strengthen internal/external relationships for future incidents.
